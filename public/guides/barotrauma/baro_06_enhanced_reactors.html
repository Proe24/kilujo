Reactor & Radiation Overhaul

Enhanced Reactors

Reactors now throw off heat and leak radiation when damaged, fuel rods are dangerous physical objects you carry by hand or with tongs, and a botched repair can melt the core down. Radiation splits into sickness you treat and contamination you scrub off.

☢Core mechanics

Reactors

  • All reactors emit heat into the surrounding room and leak radiation scaled to how damaged they are.
  • Critical state: repairing while the reactor is still on/lit triggers ~30 seconds of constant self-damage. Spam-repair (it takes no fail-damage in this state) until condition is recovering; have a crewmate with a fire extinguisher ready.
  • Meltdown size scales with the fuel rod inside — volatile fulgurium rods make the biggest blast.
  • Wrecks (including modded ones) can spawn with fuel rods in their reactors, irradiating the wreck interior.
  • On modded subs without reactor light sprites, "off" can be hard to read — verify output is zero and the reactor is actually toggled off.

Radiation — two separate things

TypeDetectionEffectCure
Radiation SicknessHidden until advanced; the early clue is burns appearing all over the body. Does NOT self-heal.Damage over time; needs active treatmentActivated Carbon Tablets (low amounts)
ContaminationOnly detectable with a Geiger CounterA contaminated person spreads radiation sickness to nearby crew (contamination itself isn't transmissible)Potassium Iodide Tablets (low amounts), or Deconsol sprayed on

You only become contaminated from a radiation source: started fuel rods, leaking reactors, radioactive ores/minerals. Radiation & heat respect distance, walls and doors — more distance and more obstacles = weaker exposure. Close doors and put walls between you and the source.

Heat

Working near a reactor, or near an active/uncontained fuel rod, slowly overheats you. Once overheating you randomly pass out and slowly burn.

Fuel rods

  • Don't stack in inventory; can only be held in hand (or moved with tongs / stored in crates).
  • Once started (by a reactor or exosuit) they glow and irradiate, are too hot to handle without gear, can start fires on the floor, and their radiation damages nearby items.
  • Started rods passively deplete over time — except inside a non-broken reactor (disabled there to preserve mod compatibility and beacon missions).
  • Crafted depleted fuel rods are NOT radioactive. Each rod type has its own radiation strength/range; fulgurium is the most dangerous.

Radioactive things: all started fuel rods (not crafted depleted ones); Uranium / Thorium / Fulgurium ores & minerals; and corpses that died contaminated (they keep irradiating — dispose of them).

▶Dealing with each mechanic, step by step

1

Repair a reactor safely

Set output to 0 / press off → wait for the lights to fully go out → only then repair. If it goes critical, spam-repair until condition recovers. Keep a fire-extinguisher buddy nearby.
2

Swap a spent rod

Equip Hazmat Suit → use Fuel Rod Tongs to pull the rod → carry to a Fuel Rod Crate (open with E on the ground) → insert. Never set an active rod on the floor. Dispose by deconstructing or loading the crate into a depth-charge loader.
3

Dive a radioactive wreck

Pre-take Potassium Iodide; wear a Hazmat Suit + O₂ tank (no pressure protection, so pair with a real diving suit if going deep); carry a Geiger Counter; minimise time and keep walls/doors between you and rods.
4

Treat crew

Geiger-check in the health menu → if contaminated, spray Deconsol and/or give Potassium Iodide → for radiation sickness give Activated Carbon Tablets (watch the burns as the warning sign). Store/remove contaminated corpses promptly.

🤖Bots & single-player

Bots can exchange fuel rods, but: equip the reactor operator with a craftable Hazmat Suit (they won't equip it themselves), and give them a "clean up items" order at higher priority than operating the reactor so they store spent rods in the correct container instead of dropping them on the floor.

⚙Add-ons & compatibility

Reactor Management Add-On (id 3354732876)

Not bot-compatible — the reactor must be player-managed. Fuel rods give less power (need multiple for full output) but last longer, and every reactor gets a module slot with four craftable modules:

  • Extra Turbine — more fuel-rod effectiveness (fewer rods for max output)
  • Extra Shielding — fully blocks radiation leaks from a damaged reactor
  • Fail-Safe CPU — prevents the critical state after a failed repair (repair while on)
  • Meltdown Preventor — fully prevents the explosion, but is destroyed doing so

Swap modules with the reactor OFF — there was a bug where swapping on a live outpost reactor caused an explosion (since fixed).

Other patches: Baroverhaul Compatibility Patch (3262062128), Hazardously Enhanced Reactors bridge (3062069071), Talent Merger Compatibility Patch (3065722159), Better Performance (3306583912, obsolete with client-side Lua).
